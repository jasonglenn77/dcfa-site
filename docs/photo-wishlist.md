# Photo & Asset Wishlist for DCFA Website Rebuild

Hi! Below is a prioritized list of photos and assets that would significantly strengthen the new website. The site is functional today using what's already on flydcfa.com, but with these additions it becomes substantially sharper than the competitor's site.

**Format guidance:** Hi-res JPG or PNG (at least 2000px on the long side). Phone photos shot in landscape with good lighting are great — they don't need to be professionally produced. If you have a Dropbox / Google Drive folder, just share the link.

---

## Tier 1 — Highest impact (would transform the site)

### 1. The fleet — one photo per aircraft
The Fleet page currently shows tail numbers and rates as text cards. With one photo per aircraft, each card becomes a real introduction.

- [ ] **N342T** (Multi-Engine Twin) — already have one good photo, but a fresh one welcome
- [ ] **N7206C** (Single-Engine)
- [ ] **N29230** (Single-Engine)
- [ ] **N555PB** (Single-Engine)
- [ ] **N38989** (Single-Engine)
- [ ] **N8650E** (Single-Engine)

Ideal: each aircraft photographed on the ramp from a 3/4 angle (you can see the side and the nose). Tail number visible. Outdoors with good light.

### 2. Instructor headshots + bios
Right now the site says "mentored by active airline pilots" but there's nobody to put a face to. Each instructor with a headshot + 2–3 sentence bio (current airline + aircraft type they fly + years instructing) would make the credibility argument concrete.

- [ ] Headshot + bio for **chief pilot / owner**
- [ ] Headshot + bio for **each CFI** (however many you have)

Ideal: relatively recent photos, framed from the chest up. Doesn't need to be in a studio — outside the hangar with the planes is great.

### 3. The hangar / facility
- [ ] **Hangar exterior** (wide shot showing the building and signage)
- [ ] **Hangar interior** (planes inside, work area visible)
- [ ] **Briefing room or classroom** (where ground school happens)
- [ ] **Front desk / lobby** (the first thing a visitor sees)

### 4. Hi-res logo (SVG strongly preferred)
The current logo is a 372×53 PNG, which is fine for header/footer but blurry when scaled up. An SVG (vector) version would render perfectly at any size and could even be used as a favicon.

- [ ] **DCFA logo as SVG** — or a hi-res PNG (at least 1500px wide)
- [ ] **Brand color hex codes** if you have them — otherwise we'll color-pick from the existing logo
- [ ] **A white-on-transparent variant** of the logo (for dark backgrounds — currently we use a CSS filter to brighten it, but a real white version would be cleaner)

---

## Tier 2 — Strong additions

### 5. Students with completion certificates
Showing real students at the moment of earning a rating (Private, Instrument, Commercial) is the most credible social proof in pilot marketing. Even a few would do.

- [ ] 3–5 photos of students holding their certificates
- [ ] First name + the rating they earned (for captions) — first name only is fine for privacy

### 6. Discovery Flight in action
- [ ] Photo of a Discovery Flight participant in the cockpit
- [ ] Photo of an instructor walking around the plane with a new student (preflight)

### 7. Testimonials (text, not photos)
Even without photos, 3–5 short quotes from former students with their first name and current airline would be powerful on the homepage. Format like:

> "DCFA got me from Discovery Flight to the right seat at [airline] in 14 months. The Self-Examining Authority is real — I never waited for a DPE."
> — **[First Name], First Officer, [Airline]**

### 8. Cockpit shots (from your own aircraft)
- [ ] G1000 / Garmin avionics close-up from one of your planes
- [ ] Wide cockpit shot showing both seats
