| 8 | Rotor Transition (RTP) | Mil. or civ. heli pilot | Custom quote | Custom | — |

**Accelerated tracks advertised:** Zero-to-Commercial in 12–14 months · Instrument-to-Commercial in 9–11 months.

### Per-program detail

**Private Pilot** — Flight 37.5 / Ground 58.5 / Pre-Post 36 / Solo 7.5. Crew concept from day one, checklist-centered procedures, airline-developed materials.

**Transition Course** — For incoming students who already hold PPL or higher. Dual single 5 / Ground 5 / Pre-Post 3. Required before aircraft rental.

**Instrument Rating (Part 141)** — Flight 47 / Ground 56.5 / Pre-Post 27.5. IMC procedures, airline-standard techniques.

**Commercial Single-Engine (Part 141)** — Single 104 / Complex/Multi 17 / Ground 54 / Pre-Post 33. Includes intro multi-engine experience.

**Multi-Engine Add-On (Part 141)** — Flight 15 / Ground 20 / Pre-Post 10. Satisfies 25 hrs actual multi for ATP.

**ATP Multi-Engine** — Flight 6.5 / Ground 20 / Pre-Post 8. Part 121 regs, airline crew protocols.

**ATP-CTP** — Four-day virtual ground school (first week of every month) + two-day sim in Dallas, TX or Las Vegas, NV. Curriculum: 30 hr virtual ground · 4 hr FTD · 6 hr full-motion Airbus A320 sim. Sim requires crew of 2; bookings <45 days out cannot guarantee sim availability. Class dates published Nov 2025 → Dec 2026.

**Rotor Transition Program (RTP)** — For military rotorcraft pilots eligible for R-ATP, and civilian commercial helicopter pilots. Military prereqs: 750 total / 200 XC / 100 night / 75 instrument / 25 multi. No entrance exam. Custom quote via onboarding form.

## 6. VA benefits

**Accepted GI Bill chapters:** 30, 33, 1606, 1607.
**VA-approved programs:** Instrument Rating · Commercial Single-Engine Land. Private Pilot is **not** VA-eligible.
**Funding window:** Aug 1 – Jul 31 annually.

**Application steps documented on current site:**
1. Obtain Certificate of Eligibility (COE) at va.gov/education/how-to-apply
2. Get Class I or II FAA medical (recommends Dr. Jerome Limoge, Colorado Springs; or FAA AME locator)
3. Submit interest form at /contact-2/

## 10. Competitor benchmark — coloradospringsflightschool.com

What they do well that we should match or beat:
- Pricing transparency on the homepage (specific dollar figures next to each pathway)
- Cost calculator tool linked from nav
- Tabbed "Learn to Fly Here" service categorization
- Active blog/briefings section with bylined staff posts
- Detailed FAQ accordion (weight limits, aircraft specs, visa eligibility, maintenance)
- Package pre-pay deals with clear prerequisites and hour breakdowns
- Multi-channel contact (phone, text, email, SMS shortcuts)
- Modern dark/navy palette, generous whitespace, card-based layout

What DCFA already has that they don't:
- Self-Examining Authority (huge — bury this nowhere)
- Active airline pilot mentorship
- Established airline partnerships (GoJet, Frontier, Key Lime)
- ATP-CTP with full-motion A320 sim
- Rotor Transition Program for military-to-airline pipeline
- VA benefits acceptance

The strategy: match their transparency and polish, then lean hard on the credentials and outcomes they can't match.
